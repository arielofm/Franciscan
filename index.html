Franciscan Anthropology

Franciscan anthropology is rooted in the teachings of the Franciscan tradition, which emphasizes a holistic understanding of humanity in relation to God, creation, and community. This perspective integrates various dimensions of human existence, including cultural, social, and spiritual aspects.

The Goal

The goal of our interest is to bring the Franciscan Tradition in dialogue in dialogue with the challenge of our time and to see how the Franciscan Tradition inspire and provoke a different or alternative sight and may change our way to be in this world.

Holistic Understanding.

Franciscan anthropology views humans as complex beings shaped by their biological, cultural, and spiritual contexts. This approach encourages a comprehensive study of humanity that includes both individual experiences and broader societal influences

Relationship with the Divine.

Central to this anthropological framework is the belief that humans are created in the image of God and possess an inherent dignity. This relationship fosters a sense of purpose and meaning in life, guiding ethical behavior and community interactions

Cultural Context.

The Franciscan tradition acknowledges the importance of cultural practices and social structures in shaping human identity. It promotes an appreciation for diversity while emphasizing shared human values that transcend cultural boundaries

Spiritual Dimension.

Franciscan anthropology also highlights the spiritual aspect of human existence. It recognizes that humans have a natural inclination towards the divine, which informs their understanding of morality, purpose, and community

complexity of human identity

Homo Faber and Homo Oeconomicus focus on material and economic aspects, while Homo Culturalis and Homo Religiosus highlight the roles of culture and spirituality in human identity.

Understanding these dimensions is crucial for a holistic view of what it means to be human in contemporary society.

Points for Reflection

Consider how your own understanding of what it means to be human aligns with the concepts of Homo Faber and Homo Oeconomicus. How might shifting towards a vision of Homo Culturalis et Religiosus influence your daily life and relationships?

Reevaluating Human Purpose

Reflect on the societal pressures to constantly produce and consume. In what ways do you see these pressures affecting your mental well-being and the health of your relationships? How might a focus on deeper desires and connections alter this dynamic?

Impact of Materialism

Think about the role of solidarity and sharing in your life. How can fostering a sense of community and prioritizing relationships over individual gain improve your interactions with others and your overall sense of fulfillment?

Value of Community

Explore the balance between material needs and spiritual well-being in your own life. How can embracing the Franciscan vision of dignity for both body and soul help you create a more harmonious existence that values both personal and communal growth?
